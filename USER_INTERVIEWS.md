# USER_INTERVIEWS

## Interview set (anonymized)

### Interview 1
- Name or initials: A.K.
- Role: Engineering Manager
- Company stage: Seed SaaS (12 employees)
- Direct quote 1: "We bought tools quickly and now we do not know what overlaps."
- Direct quote 2: "Seat count drift is our biggest hidden leak."
- Direct quote 3: "I trust recommendations only when the math is visible."
- Most surprising thing they said: They were paying for both team and individual plans for similar use.
- What this changed in product/design decisions: Added stronger overlap explanations and confidence labels in results.

### Interview 2
- Name or initials: R.M.
- Role: Founder/CEO
- Company stage: Series A B2B startup
- Direct quote 1: "I do not want another dashboard unless it gives me a clear action list."
- Direct quote 2: "Show me monthly and annual numbers side-by-side."
- Direct quote 3: "If I can share a report link with ops in one click, this becomes useful."
- Most surprising thing they said: They preferred fewer recommendations with higher confidence over long lists.
- What this changed in product/design decisions: Prioritized ranked recommendations and one-click share/export actions.

### Interview 3
- Name or initials: S.P.
- Role: Finance Lead
- Company stage: Growth startup (60 employees)
- Direct quote 1: "We need audit speed under two minutes or no one will complete it."
- Direct quote 2: "Benchmarks help, but only if cohort definitions are obvious."
- Direct quote 3: "I need provenance notes before I present this to leadership."
- Most surprising thing they said: They cared more about defensibility than absolute savings amount.
- What this changed in product/design decisions: Added provenance/explanation blocks and emphasized deterministic rule outputs.

## Question set used

1. What AI tools does your company pay for?
2. Do you know exactly how much each costs per month?
3. Have you ever tried to optimize or reduce that spend?
4. What would make you trust a free tool that tells you to switch tools?
