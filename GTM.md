# GTM

## Exact target user

The primary user is a **Head of Engineering or Technical Founder at a seed-to-Series A B2B SaaS company (8-60 employees)** with 6-30 people touching AI tools weekly. This user usually approves tooling informally on company cards, has partial visibility into who is paying for what, and is under pressure to reduce burn without slowing shipping velocity. They are not asking for another procurement platform; they want a fast, defensible answer to: "What can we cut this month without hurting output?"

## What they are doing right before they need SpendLens

They are typically in one of these moments: (1) preparing monthly spend review for founder/finance sync, (2) reacting to a surprise card statement increase from AI subscriptions or API bills, or (3) planning budget trim before hiring freeze extension. Search/scroll behavior around this moment includes queries like "Cursor vs Copilot for teams", "Claude Team pricing seats", "how to reduce AI tooling spend startup", and "SaaS burn multiple calculator". On social, they engage with threads where founders share "tool stack cost breakdown" screenshots or complain about duplicate subscriptions.

## Where they hang out online (specific)

1. r/SaaS and r/startups (cost optimization and tooling stack threads)
2. Indie Hackers (build-in-public cost and margin discussions)
3. YC founder circles and alumni Slack groups
4. CTO Craft and Engineering Leadership Slack communities
5. X lists that track startup operators discussing AI infra/tooling
6. Product Hunt maker communities where launch post-mortems include cost lessons

## First 100 users in 30 days with $0 paid budget

1. Publish a concrete "AI tooling waste teardown" post using 3 anonymized audit examples (before/after monthly savings) and link a free sample report.
2. Run direct founder outreach: 20 highly targeted DMs/day for 10 days to seed/Series A technical founders with a one-sentence hook: "If you use 3+ AI tools, this usually finds duplicate spend in under 2 minutes." 
3. Submit on Indie Hackers and Hacker News with transparent math, not a marketing pitch.
4. Offer a free "team spend snapshot" for communities (Slack/Discord) in exchange for feedback and optional testimonial.
5. Add a referral trigger in results page: "Share this report with one operator and unlock benchmark preview." 

Target funnel for first 100 users:
- 250 landing visits
- 140 audit starts
- 100 completed audits
- 25 email captures
- 8 consultation calls

## Unfair distribution channel

Credex can combine audit completion intent with infrastructure advisory credibility. Most simple calculators can only output generic plan downgrades; Credex can pair recommendation logic with implementation follow-through (contracts, migration sequencing, and procurement support). That lets the tool function as both acquisition and qualification with unusually high signal.

## Week-1 traction if this works

Week-1 success thresholds:
- 35+ completed audits
- 20%+ email capture from completed audits
- 6+ founder/operator conversations triggered from report shares
- 2+ warm consultation bookings
- At least 3 repeat visits from the same teams within 7 days

If those metrics are hit, distribution is likely resonating and the product is creating real decision value rather than novelty clicks.
