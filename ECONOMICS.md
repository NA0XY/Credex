# ECONOMICS

## Converted lead value (with assumptions shown)

Assume SpendLens is a top-of-funnel qualifier for Credex consulting + credit purchase. Use a conservative blended deal model:
- 70% of converted customers buy a $3,000 initial package
- 30% of converted customers buy a $10,000 package
- Weighted average contract value = (0.7 * 3000) + (0.3 * 10000) = **$5,100**

Assume blended contribution margin after delivery and support is 22%.
- Contribution per converted customer = 5100 * 0.22 = **$1,122**

So one converted lead is worth about **$1.1k contribution** (not top-line revenue).

## CAC by channel from GTM plan

For a $0 paid-media first month, CAC is mostly founder time + content production overhead.
Assume founder time is valued at $40/hour.

1. Founder DMs/outreach
- 35 hours/month total effort
- Cost basis: 35 * 40 = $1,400
- If this yields 8 converted customers: CAC = 1400 / 8 = **$175**

2. Community launches (HN, Indie Hackers, Slack groups)
- 20 hours/month
- Cost basis: 20 * 40 = $800
- If this yields 5 converted customers: CAC = 800 / 5 = **$160**

3. Content teardown + referral loop
- 25 hours/month
- Cost basis: 25 * 40 = $1,000
- If this yields 4 converted customers: CAC = 1000 / 4 = **$250**

Blended CAC across these channels at 17 conversions total:
- Total cost basis = 1400 + 800 + 1000 = $3,200
- Blended CAC = 3200 / 17 = **$188**

Against $1,122 contribution per converted customer, unit economics are positive with room for error.

## Conversion chain needed for profitability

Define funnel:
- Audit completed -> Consultation booked -> Credit purchase

Example profitable baseline:
- 1,000 completed audits
- 18% consultation booking from completed audits = 180 calls
- 20% close rate from calls to credit purchase = 36 customers
- Contribution = 36 * 1122 = **$40,392**

If acquisition + operating cost to generate those 1,000 audits is <$40k, this is contribution-positive. At blended CAC $188, acquisition for 36 customers costs ~$6,768, leaving a strong contribution buffer.

## What must be true to reach $1M ARR in 18 months

Target ARR = $1,000,000.
At $5,100 average contract value, annualized customers needed ˜ 196.
Monthly run-rate equivalent ˜ 17 new customers/month sustained.

Work backward using 20% call-to-close:
- Need 85 consultation calls/month
With 18% audit-to-call:
- Need ~472 completed audits/month

That is achievable only if:
1. Completion rate stays high (>=55%)
2. Result pages remain shareable and trustworthy (driving organic referrals)
3. Sales handoff from audit report to consult booking is fast (<24h response)
4. Benchmarks/re-audits create repeat usage and inbound intent

If any of those fail, growth stalls before ARR target. The model is sensitive most to consultation booking rate; improving that by even 3-5 points materially reduces required top-of-funnel volume.
